HOW TO START
─────────────

1. Put all 6 files in one folder:
   main.py, gui.py, logic.py, player.py, deck.py, card.py
1. Install the required library (first time only):
   pip install Pillow
1. Open Terminal or Command Prompt
1. Navigate to your folder:
   cd Desktop/hearts_final
1. Run the game:
   python main.py
1. The game window opens. Type your name, choose 50 or 100 Points,
   then click Start Game.

THE MENU
─────────
Start Game    → Enter your name, pick score limit, and begin
Rules         → Full rules popup
Statistics    → See your win/loss record and best score
Exit          → Close the game

YOUR NAME
──────────
Type your name before starting. It shows on the table and
saves with your stats so your record is linked to you.

SCORE LIMIT
────────────
50 Points  — shorter game (around 20 minutes)
100 Points — longer game  (around 45 minutes)

The game ends the moment any player reaches the limit.
The player with the LOWEST score wins.

THE TABLE
──────────
AI 2 ★ sits at the TOP    (Smart AI — may try Shoot the Moon)
AI 1   sits on the LEFT
AI 3   sits on the RIGHT
You    sit at the BOTTOM

Centre of the table shows the cards played this trick.
Left panel shows total scores, round scores, and game status.
Right panel shows the full move log as the game plays out.

The dealer label shows who is dealing this round.
The dealer rotates clockwise after every round.

ANIMATIONS
───────────
When the game starts, cards are shuffled and dealt automatically.
Watch each card fly from the deck to the player seats.

When any player plays a card, it flies to the centre of the
table and flips over so you can see what was played.

When someone wins a trick, all 4 cards fly to their side.
A banner shows who won the trick.

These animations happen for every player including the AI.

YOUR HAND
──────────
Your 13 cards are shown at the bottom of the screen.

Gold highlight = you CAN play this card (click to play)
Grey card      = not allowed right now

Valid cards are raised slightly so they are easy to spot.
Hover your mouse over any card to see the full card name
and how many penalty points it is worth.

PLAYING A CARD
───────────────
Option 1: Click a gold highlighted card
Option 2: Press Enter to play the first valid card automatically
Option 3: Click “Valid Cards” button to see a list of allowed cards

PAUSE AND RESUME
─────────────────
Click the orange Pause button at any point to stop the game.
A dark overlay appears on the table with a PAUSED message.
Click Resume (same button) or press Space to continue.
The game picks up exactly where you left off.

RULES SUMMARY
──────────────
Who goes first:
The player holding the 2 of Clubs always goes first.
That player must play the 2 of Clubs — no other choice.

Follow Suit:
The first card played sets the suit for that round.
If the first card is a Spade, you must play a Spade
if you have one. Same rule applies to all suits.

No matching suit:
If you have no cards of the lead suit, you can play
anything. This is your chance to dump penalty cards
onto someone else’s trick.

Hearts Lead:
You cannot start a round with a Heart until Hearts are
broken. Hearts break the moment anyone plays a Heart.
Once broken, Hearts can be led freely for the rest of
that hand. Hearts reset at the start of every new hand.

Only Hearts left:
If every card in your hand is a Heart, you must play
one even if Hearts are not broken yet. No penalty for
this — it is an official rule.

Scoring:
♥  Each Heart card  = 1 penalty point
♠  Queen of Spades  = 5 penalty points
All other cards     = 0 points
Total per round     = 18 points maximum
Lowest score WINS

Shoot the Moon:
If one player collects ALL 13 Hearts and the Queen of
Spades in one hand (all 18 points):
→ That player scores 0 for the round
→ Everyone else gets +18 added to their score
Watch AI 2 ★ — it switches to this strategy when it
is holding enough penalty cards.

AI PLAYERS
───────────
AI 1 and AI 3 — Basic defensive strategy
They avoid winning tricks that have penalty cards.
They dump the Queen of Spades on others when they can.
They play the lowest card they can get away with.

AI 2 ★ — Smart AI
Normally plays defensively like AI 1 and AI 3.
But if it collects enough penalty cards, it switches
strategy and starts trying to win every single trick.
It is going for Shoot the Moon.
This can change the game dramatically — if it succeeds,
everyone else gets 18 points at once.

BETWEEN ROUNDS
───────────────
After all 13 tricks are played, scores are updated.
The round summary shows points gained and total scores.
Click Next Round to shuffle and deal fresh cards.
The shuffle and deal animation plays again.
The game continues until someone hits the score limit.

KEYBOARD SHORTCUTS
───────────────────
Enter   →  Play the first valid card automatically
Space   →  Pause or Resume the game
Escape  →  Quit (asks for confirmation first)

STATISTICS
───────────
Your stats are saved automatically after every completed game.
Go to Statistics from the main menu to see:
- Your name
- Total games played
- Games won and lost
- Your best score ever (lowest total)

You can reset your stats from the Statistics screen.
Stats are linked to the last name you played with.

FILES CREATED WHILE PLAYING
─────────────────────────────
scores.json  — your win/loss record, loaded every time you play
game_log.txt — full record of every card played with timestamps
cards/       — folder of card images, created automatically

TROUBLESHOOTING
────────────────
“No module named PIL” or “No module named Pillow”
→ Run:  pip install Pillow
→ Then run the game again

“python: command not found”
→ Download Python 3 from https://python.org
→ Make sure Python is added to your system PATH

Game window does not open
→ Make sure all 6 files are in the SAME folder
→ Always run main.py, not gui.py or any other file

Cards folder missing or cards look wrong
→ Delete the cards/ folder if it exists
→ Run the game again — it will rebuild automatically

Game freezes during animation
→ Wait a moment, animations complete on their own
→ Do not click repeatedly during animations

Scores not saving
→ Make sure you finish a complete game (someone hits the limit)
→ Stats only save when a full game is completed
